WHAT WE'RE TESTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Can a 5-minute voice recording detect anhedonia as accurately as a $500 brain scan?

Stream A is the acoustic half. You extract features from clinical interview audio
to build a classifier that predicts anhedonia severity.

YOUR PIPELINE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Dataset: DAIC-WOZ (~189 clinical interview recordings + PHQ-8 scores)

Step 1 — TRANSCRIPTION (Whisper large-v3)
  Raw audio → text + word timestamps
  Handles: disfluencies, pauses, overlapping speech

Step 2 — SPEAKER DIARIZATION (Pyannote 3.1)
  Separates interviewer vs. patient audio
  You analyze only the PATIENT's speech, not the virtual agent (Ellie)

Step 3 — ACOUSTIC FEATURE EXTRACTION (OpenSMILE eGeMAPSv02)
  88 features: pitch variability (CV_F0), energy flux (CV_Energy),
  speaking rate, jitter, shimmer, formant frequencies
  Standard benchmark for clinical speech research (AVEC challenges)

Output: stream_a_features.csv — 189 rows × 88 acoustic features + PHQ-8 score
