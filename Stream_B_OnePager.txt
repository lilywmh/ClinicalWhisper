WHAT WE'RE TESTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Can a 5-minute voice recording detect anhedonia as accurately as a $500 brain scan?

Stream B is the neural gold standard. You extract brain activation from fMRI scans
to build a classifier that predicts anhedonia — this is what Stream A has to beat.

YOUR PIPELINE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Dataset: ds000030 (~272 subjects, UCLA — fMRI scans during a reward task)
Source: OpenNeuro — publicly available, no access request needed
URL: https://openneuro.org/datasets/ds000030

Step 1 — PREPROCESSING (fMRIPrep 23.x)
  Raw .nii brain scans → motion-corrected, spatially normalized scans
  Template: MNI152NLin2009cAsym at 2mm resolution
  ⚡ FIRST: Check if pre-processed derivatives exist on OpenNeuro
     If yes → skip this step entirely (saves ~800 CPU-hours and 2 weeks)

Step 2 — ROI EXTRACTION (Nilearn)
  Focus: Nucleus Accumbens (NAcc) + dorsal striatum (reward centers)
  Mask: Harvard-Oxford subcortical atlas
  Task contrast: BART — cash-out > inflate (reward processing)

Step 3 — QUALITY CONTROL
  Exclude if: mean framewise displacement (FD) > 0.5mm
  Exclude if: >20% frames censored or failed spatial normalization
  Visual QC: carpet plots, motion distributions, registration montages

Output: stream_b_features.csv — per-subject NAcc/striatum BOLD values + anhedonia measure
